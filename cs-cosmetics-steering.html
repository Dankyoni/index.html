Project Summary

China Cosmetics Re-Analysis & Mid-Term Plan Review — STAR

This is the same case I use in interviews (Requirement ① — issue structuring & management recommendations): not a market report, but re-framing the client’s China business environment so leadership could decide where to invest in 2025.

Situation
In 2024, China saw weak consumer spending and a prestige slowdown. A global beauty manufacturer needed to re-analyze its China business and run a semi-annual mid-term strategy review — twice in one year.
Task
As project lead, I had to turn the re-analysis into investment decision materials — structured so China leadership could debate portfolio, channel, and resource allocation for 2025.
Action
Organized 2024 market in four layers — macro · category · channel · competition (Euromonitor + syndicated EC data). H1: 2025 market outlook. H2: validated the outlook and presented 2025 investment direction. Concretely: high prestige → hero SKU focus; mass → local development to accelerate product launch cycles; channels — e.g. Tier 2–3 city external distribution and more offline new-customer touchpoints. Moved away from equal investment across all brand lines to ranked portfolio · channel · resource priorities.
Result
Two annual management reviews: H1 set the outlook; H2 connected verification and policy revision — linking early outlook to course correction. Output served as a management policy draft supporting 2025 mid-term plan investment decisions.
Context

Why a Mid-Term Review — Not a One-Off Report

Market
Cosmetics growth slowing (~4–5%); prestige under pressure; relative strength in masstige / high prestige. EC already 40%+ of sales — Tmall vs live commerce dynamics mattered. Japanese-brand sentiment added headwind in parts of the category.
Client ask
H1 (Jun · ~49 slides): 2025 market outlook from the 2024 four-layer re-analysis. H2 (Nov · ~54 slides): validate H1 assumptions after 618 / Double 11 and competitor moves — then present 2025 investment direction and update priorities entering the plan cycle.
Core issue
Equal brand-line investment was inefficient. Leadership needed explicit ranking across portfolio, channel, and budget — not another category overview.

Four Layers → Three Recommendation Pillars

Both decks shared the same analytical spine. The deliverable was where to invest in 2025, expressed as three levers leadership could act on.

① Segment
Role-based portfolio allocation
High prestige → hero SKU cultivation (base makeup as new-user entry). Mass skincare → local development to accelerate product launch cycles. Mass UV → compare cross-border EC vs local production routes.
② Channel
Tier 2–3 & offline expansion
External distribution in Tier 2–3 cities and more offline new-customer touchpoints — alongside core EC routes. H2 validated which channel bets held after peak-season actuals.
③ Resource
Priority ranking
Exit equal brand-line logic → confirm order across portfolio · channel · budget. Reinforce hero SKU + functional / evidence-based claims across segments.
