Situation

Revising China Mid-Term Strategy Under Market Pressure

Context
China cosmetics demand remained under pressure in 2024: weak consumer confidence, prestige slowdown, and Japanese-brand sentiment headwinds — while EC (especially TikTok) and lower-tier cities showed selective growth pockets. Client China leadership needed to revisit the mid-term plan, not just refresh market data.
The Ask
Two steering cycles in 2024: H1 (Jun) to set the outlook and forward investment hypotheses for 2025; H2 (Nov) to test those hypotheses against H1 actuals, competitive moves, and 618 / Double 11 signals — then update portfolio and resource priorities entering 2025.
My Role
Project lead synthesizing Euromonitor, syndicated EC, and macro sources into executive steering decks — structuring issues, presenting options, and translating analysis into portfolio · channel · resource priority recommendations that became the basis for management discussion.

Step 01 — Issue Structuring

Four-Layer Analysis to Reframe the Planning Question

Both steering packs shared a common analytical spine. The goal was not category reporting for its own sake, but to answer: where should China portfolio, channel, and resource investment concentrate in 2025?

Competitive & Client Implications
Layer Key Issues Structured Planning Implication
Macro GDP path · policy stimulus narrative · tier-city income divergence (Tier 3–4 vs Tier 1–2) Demand recovery uneven — portfolio cannot assume one national growth curve
Category Skincare / makeup / fragrance / hair / dermocosmetics · Mass Premium vs High Prestige Prestige under pressure · masstige and science-backed segments relatively stronger
Channel Retail vs EC penetration · Tmall vs TikTok dynamics · 618 / Double 11 direction EC 40%+ structurally — channel roles must be split by acquisition vs repeat
Competitive International vs local strategies · Japanese-brand stress · EC incubation (Douyin flagship, KOL/livestream) Competitors accelerating on product strength + efficiency — client needs ranked priorities, not equal SKU spend
Core diagnosis: equal investment across all SKUs was structurally inefficient. The mid-term plan review had to move leadership toward explicit portfolio, channel, and resource ranking.
Steps 02–03 — Options & Priorities

From Strategic Options to Portfolio · Channel · Resource Ranking

Recommendations were organized across four axes — consumer, product, channel, communication — then translated into three priority levers leadership could act on.

Priority 01 · Segment
Portfolio role allocation
High prestige: concentrate on hero SKU cultivation · Mass skincare: accelerate via local development · Mass UV: evaluate cross-border EC vs local production routes
Priority 02 · Channel
Purpose-based channel split
Acquisition: Redbook · hotel / lifestyle touchpoints · live commerce · Repeat: Tmall flagship · membership repurchase · H2 update: upward revision to TikTok / live resource allocation
Priority 03 · Resource
Ranked investment order
Exit equal-SKU logic → confirm order across portfolio · channel · budget · reinforce hero SKU + functional / evidence-based claims across segments

Result

Management Decision Materials — Not a Report That Stopped at Analysis

Outcome
Two executive steering conversations in 2024 aligned China leadership on the outlook (H1), then on course correction and 2025 priorities (H2) — supporting the client's China mid-term plan review and investment decision process.
Decision value
Quantified why EC + lower-tier cities + masstige / science positioning matter while prestige and Japanese-brand cohorts face headwinds — and translated that into ranked portfolio, channel, and resource choices leadership could act on.
Execution link
Outputs served as the launchpad for execution: brand owners, EC teams, and local functions could proceed from agreed priorities rather than reopening unstructured debate — with H2 providing the validation loop for field adjustment.
