China New Product Development:
From Category Research to Executive Decision

Coordinated a 6-month NPD program for a global household & personal care manufacturer — from market sizing and consumer research to a 61-slide executive steering pack that shaped the client's China product roadmap.

ClientGlobal household & personal care manufacturer (Japan HQ)

Deliverable~61-slide executive capstone pack

01 — Situation

The Brief

A global household and personal care manufacturer needed a structured NPD funnel across six China categories — not ad-hoc SKU ideas, but evidence-backed directional concepts ready for FY budget decisions. Category and sales leadership required hard facts on market trajectory, online channel structure, competitive models, and internal gaps before committing development budget.

The program sat inside a weekly PMO cadence, requiring triangulation of Tmall platform data, client EC analytics, consumer insight, regulatory constraints, and ODM feasibility — alongside 4+ parallel client engagements at NRI Shanghai.

03 — Approach

Two-Stage Concept Funnel

The program used a structured two-stage funnel to convert an open-ended list of 20+ SKU ideas into ~10 directional concepts with explicit trade-offs — ready for FY planning decisions.

01
Primary Screening
Normalized renovation + net-new ideas into a single hypothesis grid by category, use-case, format & development difficulty
02
Market Evidence
Tmall GMV, CAGR, price-band distribution, brand concentration, consumer SNS signals & pricing surveys per category
03
Competitor Study
Brand maps, 4P hypotheses, channel economics & client positioning gaps vs. EC-native domestic competitors

Directional SKUs with feasibility vs. market attractiveness trade-offs for FY planning
Evidence principle: Triangulate syndicated/platform data, client EC proxies, primary surveys, public flagship listings, expert/ODM landscape, and regulatory desk research — no single-source SKU bets.
04 — Key Findings

What the Research Revealed

Across six categories, four themes consistently shaped the strategic recommendations:

Market Structure
Aroma is the highest-priority category
CAGR 15% · Largest Tmall GMV across 6 categories
No Japanese brand holds a dominant position in premium aroma — creating a clear gap for a Japanese quality + air-care expert positioning strategy.
Competitive Gap
EC-native domestic brands set the pace
Client: ~80% paid search · Rivals: KOL-first content model
Top competitors run content-first traffic models with fast product iteration — limiting social/SNS reach vs. EC-native rivals.
Consumer Insight
Usage occasion and scent story drive conversion
4 occasions: bedroom · closet · car · pet-care
Scent storytelling and lifestyle-led KOL content outperformed generic deodorization claims in awareness-to-conversion.
Regulatory
Claims architecture matters before page creation
PRC Ad Law: sleep / aroma efficacy claims restricted pre-page
Front-loading the claims architecture — including test-institution citation paths — prevented costly page-creation rework downstream.
