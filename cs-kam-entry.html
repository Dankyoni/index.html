Situation

A Competitive Bid for Exclusive Distribution Rights in China

Background
A Japanese specialty consumer brand in the color care category sought an exclusive general-agent to introduce its product line to the China market. Three agencies competed for the mandate — including two established players with existing China distribution networks.
The Work
Built and presented the complete market entry proposal: category and regulatory investigation, brand positioning for China, commercial architecture (pricing, channel, distribution model), P&L simulation, and negotiation with senior Japanese client executives through to internal approval.
Outcome
Won the exclusive general-agent mandate within six months of joining the organization. Subsequently built the China commercial architecture from zero — controlled distribution, price floors, channel strategy — and managed the account as KAM.

Market Context

China Cross-Border Color Care: A Category at Inflection

The China cross-border color care shampoo category was in rapid growth at the time of the bid — but the competitive dynamics revealed structural risk. The Japanese pioneer brand that had entered early and held market leadership was losing share. Meanwhile, European challengers (Italian and Spanish brands) were scaling aggressively via heavy traffic investment, and a Hong Kong entrant was competing on price. The window for a well-resourced distributor to defend the brand's position was closing.

The competitive analysis built into the bid was not a neutral market report — it was a case for urgency. Without a committed, operationally capable general agent to rebuild distribution discipline and brand investment, the client's early-mover advantage would erode as the category commoditized.
Approach — 4-Stage Bid Process

From Market Intelligence to Executive Approval

Stage 01
Market & Regulatory Investigation
Category sizing, competitive landscape, regulatory scope (NMPA, cross-border import), distribution channel structure
Stage 02
Brand Positioning for China
Target consumer definition, pricing tier placement, competitive differentiation vs. domestic and imported alternatives
Stage 03
Commercial Case & P&L Model
Joint business model with revenue scenarios, margin simulation, pricing floors, multi-year P&L and cash-flow assumptions
Stage 04
Negotiation & Internal Approval
Presentation and negotiation with senior Japanese client executives; aligned on commercial terms, distribution rights, and activation timeline
The differentiator in the bid was not just the market case — it was the commercial architecture specificity: price floors, controlled distribution logic, and a co-branded trade investment model. Generic market reports lose to structured commercial proposals.

Ongoing KAM — After the Win

From Mandate to Account Ownership

Winning the bid was the entry point. The real work was building a structured KAM relationship that made the Japan principal confident their brand was being managed strategically in China — not just distributed.

Regular Reporting Cadence
Monthly sell-in / sell-out reports with China market commentary; quarterly business reviews aligning on channel performance, promotional ROI, and portfolio priorities with Japan HQ stakeholders.
Joint Business Planning
Annual and seasonal JBP covering volume forecasts, promotional investment, channel activation calendar, and brand protection measures — co-developed with Japan brand team and internal operations.
Channel Development
Expanded from initial cross-border e-commerce entry into offline specialty retail and professional salon channels; prioritized placements that reinforced the brand's quality positioning vs domestic alternatives.
Brand Protection
Monitored and acted on unauthorized importers; price-floor enforcement via sub-distributor agreements; escalated brand integrity issues to Japan principal with documented channel audits.
The shift from bid winner to trusted KAM partner required consistent delivery against JBP commitments and transparent reporting — even when results required course correction. Client trust at the C-level principal relationship level was built through cadence and accountability, not just commercial outcomes.
Output & Skills Demonstrated

Competitive Win + Full-Cycle Account Ownership

This engagement demonstrates the full KAM and BD cycle: competitive intelligence and bid strategy → commercial architecture design → executive negotiation → ongoing account management and Japan HQ relationship. The market entry case built here directly informed subsequent brand portfolio work across the wider account book.
